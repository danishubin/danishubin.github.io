USCF Rules can be found here and FIDE Rules can be found here.

There are several different levels a chess player can go through. To find out more, visit titles.

For each player, the tournament they should play in is different. However, a simplified plan would be that beginners play in NW chess tournaments and intermediate players play in USCF rate tournaments. Advanced players should play in a mix of USCF and FIDE rated tournaments. To find out more email Coach Emil.

As you all know, their are many different time controls. However, Coach Emil reccomends games at least G/45 and all the way up to G/90. This allows the player to think and record the game.

Yes, the Nationals Prep classes are available for all students and levels.

Chess has many benefits and very few cons. Overall, this board game helps your child grow and be happier. To find out just some of the benefits read this article. If you found that one intersting, take a look at this journal as well.

FIDE is the rating system used internationally, USCF is used nationally, and NW chess is used locally (mostly Washington, Oregon, and Idaho).
